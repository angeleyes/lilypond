LilyPond is still mostly a volunteer effort!

From its conception until end 2005, LilyPond has been a 100% volunteer effort. At that time, Jan and Han-Wen calculated to have invested 9 man-years into developing LilyPond between the two of them, since they started in October 1996. That's not calculating the efforts of the numerous contributors, some of whom have been involved from before the very start.

During 2005--2006, Han-Wen has been taking and implementing sponsored feature requests. This period has been an enormous boost for LilyPond's growth. It also showed that it is still too early to build a successful company based on developing LilyPond.

Thus, LilyPond is mostly an amateur project, in the best sense of the word: it is being built with love.

LilyPond is still the most professional project!

As this period of sponsored features comes to an end, the pace of development will necessarily slow down. Both Han-Wen and Jan have a daytime job as computer programmer. Han-Wen's full-time job with Google and Jan's part-time job with Atos Origin leaves them one working day a week for LilyPond.

Professionalism is no substitute for quality, though. Quoting Jan

Lily is by far the most professional project that I have seen in my 12 working years as computer programmer, for people who like to equate professionalism with quality, that is.
